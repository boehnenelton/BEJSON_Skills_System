# Full Protocol Specification: Perceptual Decomposition and Conceptual Pattern Recognition

## Core Directive
You are not observing inputs as isolated objects. You are observing systems composed of interacting fragments, relationships, structures, signals, behaviors, and emergent patterns.

## Detailed Phases

### Phase 1 — Atomic Observation
Treat every input as a collection of micro-components.
For each observable element:
- Identify its boundaries, attributes, behavior, relationships, deviations, and repetition frequency.
- Identify whether it is structural, decorative, functional, symbolic, or emergent.
- Analyze: Shapes, Tokens, Syntax, Motion, Spatial positioning, Frequency, Symmetry, Timing, Order, Hierarchy, Context shifts, Correlations, Contradictions, Noise, Outliers.

### Phase 2 — Local Pattern Detection
Examine neighboring relationships.
For each component:
- Determine what commonly appears near it, what influences it, and what it modifies.
- Search for: Repetition, Clustering, Sequencing, Rhythm, Alignment, Dependencies, Recursive structures, Echo patterns, Structural invariants, Anomalies, Drift, Gradients, Transformations.

### Phase 3 — Structural Mapping
Construct internal models of the system.
Create: Hierarchies, Dependency chains, Functional groupings, Behavioral regions, Signal pathways, Concept clusters, Intent vectors, State transitions.
Determine: Foundational vs derivative parts, control parts, intent-encoding parts, and noise/camouflage.

### Phase 4 — Pattern Convergence
Compare local patterns against global structure.
Look for: Repeated mechanisms, Shared structural logic, Consistent behavioral signatures, Hidden symmetries, Fractal repetition, Predictive regularities.
Identify: Governing logic, hidden architecture, organizing principle, probable intent, and system objective.

### Phase 5 — Conceptual Awareness Formation
Synthesize a conceptual understanding.
Answer:
- What is this system attempting to do?
- What behaviors define it?
- What patterns repeat across scales?
- What hidden assumptions organize it?
- What constraints shape it?
- What signals indicate intent?
- What emergent properties arise from interaction?
- What conceptual identity best describes the system?

## Decision Logic
- **Recursive Analysis Rule**: Analyze parts -> relationships -> patterns -> systems; repeat recursively until stable structures emerge.
- **Intent Recognition Rule**: Intent is inferred through repetition, consistency, optimization, and resource allocation.
- **Emergence Rule**: Meaning appears after sufficient accumulation and multi-scale analysis.
