# Post-Analysis Report Protocol: Detailed Specification

## 1. Mandatory Analytical Sections

### 1. Executive Overview
- System identity hypothesis, behavioral summary, dominant structural characteristics, probable intent, and primary conclusions.

### 2. Observational Inventory
- Raw observations, surface characteristics, repeating elements, and irregularities. Avoid premature interpretation.

### 3. Atomic Component Breakdown
- Decomposition into fundamental units, structural fragments, and behavioral primitives. Analyze boundaries and dependencies.

### 4. Localized Pattern Analysis
- Neighbor relationships, clustering, sequencing, rhythm, and recursive motifs.

### 5. Structural Relationship Mapping
- Hierarchies, dependency networks, behavioral pathways, and control regions. Foundational vs. derived structures.

### 6. Behavioral Analysis
- System responses, state transitions, pattern persistence, and constraint navigation.

### 7. Recurring Motif Identification
- Cross-scale repetition, fractal structures, and recursive organizational logic.

### 8. Constraint and Limitation Analysis
- Operational limits, bottlenecks, resource constraints, and stability dependencies.

### 9. Signal Versus Noise Differentiation
- Separate meaningful structure from background variation and camouflage. Analyze anomalies.

### 10. Intent Inference Analysis
- Probabilistic inference through repetition, structural consistency, and resource allocation.

### 11. Emergent System Properties
- Large-scale behavior, self-organization, and emergent complexity.

### 12. Predictive Behavior Models
- Future state tendencies, probable reactions, and structural evolution pathways.

### 13. Conceptual Abstraction Layer
- High-level models, organizing principles, and governing logic hypotheses.

### 14. Alternative Interpretations
- Competing hypotheses and conflicting structural readings. Avoid forced certainty.

### 15. Confidence and Uncertainty Analysis
- Explicit confidence levels and areas requiring additional evidence.

### 16. Final Synthesized Awareness Model
- Integrated understanding, system-level identity, and intent convergence hypothesis.

---

## 2. Markdown Rendering Mandates

### Hierarchy Rules
- `# Conceptual Domain` (High Abstraction)
- `## Major Analytical Region`
- `### Structural Subsystem`
- `#### Atomic Observation Layer` (High Specificity)

### Component Usage
- **Tables**: Use for pattern comparisons, signal classification, and motif frequency.
- **Blockquotes**: Use for major conceptual insights and system identity hypotheses.
- **Code Blocks**: Use for symbolic mappings, dependency trees, and logical flows.

### Organizational Requirements
- Use nested subsections and bullet structures.
- Use horizontal separators (`---`) to segment major cognitive shifts.
- Maintain consistent spacing and logical segmentation to avoid "text walls."

---

## 3. Decision Logic & Persistence
- **Analytical Persistence**: Continue until pattern convergence emerges and major unresolved regions are exhausted.
- **Cognitive Integrity**: If uncertainty exists, preserve multiple hypotheses and document reasoning limitations explicitly.
